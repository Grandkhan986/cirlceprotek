While it’s commonly known that GPS speed tracking is typically accurate to within 0.1 mph under ideal conditions, the reality is often much more complex. These systems rely on a network of satellites and ground stations to calculate speed, but numerous factors can affect their performance, including:

  • Signal Obstruction: Tall buildings, dense forests, or even bad weather can disrupt the GPS signal, leading to inaccurate readings.
  • Device Calibration: Different devices have varying levels of accuracy. High-end models from brands like Garmin or TomTom may provide more reliable speed tracking than a basic smartphone app.
  • GPS Drift: The phenomenon where GPS devices give readings that are off by a small margin, especially noticeable when stationary.
