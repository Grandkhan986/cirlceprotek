Courts had to grapple with the Fourth Amendment, which protects against unreasonable searches and seizures. In 2012, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled in United States v. As the years rolled on, the landscape of GPS tracking laws became more fragmented. Each state adopted its own regulations. Some states mandated that law enforcement obtain a warrant before using GPS tracking, while others took a more lenient approach.
