This piecemeal approach can leave gaps in privacy, allowing some apps to continue tracking while others are restricted. So, how can you effectively stop your iPhone from tracking you?

  • Review App Permissions: Go to Settings > Privacy > Location Services and assess each app’s access. Disable location tracking for apps that don’t need it to function.
  • Use “While Using the App” Setting: Instead of allowing apps to access your location “Always,” choose “While Using the App” to limit data sharing.
  • Disable Significant Locations: In Settings > Privacy > Location Services > System Services, turn off Significant Locations to stop your iPhone from keeping a history of where you've been.
  • Turn Off Share My Location: If you don’t need it, disable this feature in Settings > Privacy > Location Services > Share My Location.
