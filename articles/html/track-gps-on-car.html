From military origins to everyday utility, this journey is as compelling as the devices themselves.

Initially developed for military navigation, Global Positioning System (GPS) technology emerged in the 1970s. It was designed to provide precise location data for defense operations. The commercial use of GPS began in the 1980s, paving the way for consumer applications. As technology advanced, GPS tracking systems began to find their place in vehicles, fundamentally altering how we navigate roads.

Fast forward to today, and the landscape has shifted dramatically. An estimated 20% of vehicles are now equipped with GPS tracking devices, whether factory-installed or added later.
