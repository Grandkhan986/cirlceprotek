In the early days, home security largely relied on physical barriers, such as locks and bolts. As crime rates began to rise in urban areas during the late 20th century, homeowners sought innovative solutions to safeguard their properties. This shift marked the beginning of a new era, where technology played a crucial role in transforming home safety.

  • 1970s: The first closed-circuit television (CCTV) systems were introduced, primarily used in commercial settings. These bulky setups were expensive and complicated, limiting their availability for everyday consumers.
  • 1990s: The advent of digital technology brought substantial changes. Homeowners began to experiment with DIY security solutions, and brands like ADT and Honeywell started offering more accessible options.
  • 2000s: The rise of the internet led to the development of IP cameras, allowing homeowners to monitor their properties remotely. This innovation was a game changer in the quest to transform safety.
  • 2010s: The introduction of smart cameras, such as those from Ring and Nest, made home surveillance user-friendly and affordable. These devices not only provided video footage but also integrated seamlessly with smartphones, allowing for real-time alerts and monitoring.
