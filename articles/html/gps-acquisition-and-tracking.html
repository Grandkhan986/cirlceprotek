The journey of GPS technology began in the 1970s as a military tool, developed by the U.S. Department of Defense. Initially, the system was designed for precise navigation of military vehicles, but as the technology matured, it became available to civilian users in the 1980s. By the late 1990s, GPS had become a household name, paving the way for its integration into everyday devices such as smartphones and dedicated GPS units.

Today, GPS tracking is not just about getting from point A to point B; it’s about enhancing every aspect of your adventure. These innovations allow adventurers to focus on the experience rather than the fear of getting lost.

Here are some key milestones in the history of GPS that highlight how it has revolutionized your adventures:

  • 1978: The first GPS satellite was launched, marking the dawn of satellite-based navigation.
  • 1983: President Reagan announced that GPS would be made available for civilian use, opening the door for personal navigation.
  • 2000: The U.S. government discontinued selective availability, improving GPS accuracy for civilian users from 100 meters to less than 10 meters.
  • 2010: The advent of smartphone GPS apps, enabling people to access navigation tools on-the-go.
