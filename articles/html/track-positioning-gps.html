The journey of GPS technology began in the early 1970s, developed by the U.S. Department of Defense. Originally designed for military applications, the Global Positioning System was made available for civilian use in the 1980s. This transition marked a significant turning point, opening the door for everyday adventurers to benefit from accurate positioning and navigation. Today, GPS technology has an estimated global market size exceeding $20 billion annually, reflecting its widespread adoption across various sectors.

As we delve into the historical context, it’s essential to recognize the milestones that have led us to this point:

  • 1978: The first GPS satellite was launched, marking the inception of a system that would change navigation forever.
  • 1983: President Reagan announced that GPS would be made available for civilian use, paving the way for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • 1995: The full constellation of 24 satellites became operational, offering global coverage and reliability.
  • 2000: The U.S. government discontinued selective availability, enhancing the accuracy of consumer GPS devices, thus revolutionizing your adventures.

Fast forward to today, GPS technology is embedded in various devices—smartphones, dedicated navigation units, and even wearables—allowing adventurers to seamlessly integrate navigation into their experiences. Brands like Garmin and TomTom have become synonymous with reliable GPS devices, enabling hikers to trek through dense forests or bikers to navigate winding trails with confidence.
