Researchers must consider vast expanses of water where signal loss can occur, making it difficult to maintain continuous tracking. Additionally, the physical installation of tracking devices on sharks can be problematic. These animals are elusive, and attaching a device can pose risks to both the sharks and the researchers.

To combat these challenges, scientists are exploring various solutions:

  • Advanced Tagging Techniques: Researchers are developing lightweight, non-invasive tracking devices that can be attached to sharks with minimal stress. These devices can store data and transmit it when the shark surfaces, ensuring that vital information is not lost.

  • Community Engagement: Local fishermen and coastal communities play a crucial role in monitoring shark populations. By educating them on the importance of these creatures and involving them in tracking efforts, researchers can gather valuable data while fostering conservation efforts.
  • Data Integration: Combining GPS tracking data with environmental factors such as water temperature and prey availability allows scientists to create comprehensive movement models. This method enhances our understanding of why and how great whites migrate.
