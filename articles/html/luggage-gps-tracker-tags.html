  • 1980s: The Birth of Barcodes - The introduction of barcode technology in the 1980s marked a significant step toward tracking luggage. Airlines began to attach barcodes to bags, allowing for better tracking through their systems. While it didn’t eliminate lost luggage, it laid the groundwork for future innovations.
  • 2000s: RFID Technology - RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) tags emerged as a game-changer. These tags allowed for real-time tracking, significantly reducing the number of lost bags. Airlines like Delta and British Airways adopted this technology, ensuring passengers could follow their luggage's journey.
  • 2010s: The Rise of Smart Trackers - With the advent of smartphones, the market saw the emergence of Bluetooth and GPS-enabled luggage trackers. Brands like Tile and LugLoc made headlines by offering devices that promised to never lose your luggage again. These trackers connected to your phone, providing real-time updates on your bag's location.
