  • Signal Timing: The device calculates how long it takes for each signal to reach it, which helps to pinpoint its exact position.

One of the biggest challenges is signal interference, which can disrupt the reliability of GPS tracking. Urban environments, with their towering buildings and dense infrastructure, can create a phenomenon known as "urban canyon," where signals bounce off structures, leading to inaccuracies. Additionally, adverse weather conditions like heavy rain or snow can affect the satellite signals. For instance, during a major storm in New York in 2021, many users reported their GPS devices failing to provide accurate locations, highlighting the limitations of even the most advanced technology.

To tackle these issues, manufacturers have made significant advancements in GPS technology. Newer devices, like those from Garmin and TomTom, integrate additional sensors such as accelerometers and gyroscopes to enhance location accuracy. This multi-sensor approach helps mitigate the impact of signal disruption, allowing for more reliable tracking even in challenging environments. Moreover, companies are increasingly utilizing augmented reality (AR) in their apps to provide users with real-time navigational assistance, further enhancing the user experience.
