Historically, birdwatchers used simple methods such as binoculars and field guides. The first documented bird banding occurred in 1902, when researchers began attaching lightweight metal bands to birds to track their movements. This rudimentary approach laid the foundation for future advancements. In the mid-20th century, radio telemetry emerged, allowing ornithologists to follow birds with radio signals, but it was cumbersome and limited in scope.

This way, even if GPS signals falter, your device can still provide location updates via cellular networks, keeping you connected no matter where your birdwatching takes you.
